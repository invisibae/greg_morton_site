Selected Work

A small sample of favorite recent stories of mine.

The Baltimore Banner Nov. 2023

Car theft rates have tripled in Baltimore. We visited the hardest-hit neighborhood.

An investigation into Baltimore's 2023 car theft crisis. The series involved a mix of public records reporting, data analysis and community engagement.

The Washington Post Sept. 2023

Lawmakers are spending way more to keep themselves safe. Is it enough?

Using a mix of data sources, I did this deep dive into the changing rules and trends around legislative security.

The Baltimore Banner May 2024

Banner analysis: Margin in white neighborhoods powered Scott to victory

My 2024 Primary Election Day coverage used linear interpolation to explore some of the race and class dynamics of the Baltimore Mayoral race.

The Baltimore Banner Oct. 2023

The 2023 O’s were historically clutch. Will it last in October?

It did not last.

The Baltimore Banner July 2024

Why does Maryland dominate at the Olympics? Look at the bank accounts.

I found my way into NBC's hidden API containing every Olympian and the entire Olympic schedule. I scraped the data and used it in combination with U.S Census data to tell a story about why Maryland is so good at producing Olympians.

The Baltimore Banner Feb. 2024

1 in 5 guns on Baltimore’s streets don’t have serial numbers. That’s a problem.

My colleague Lee Sanderlin and I were the first reporters to examine the Baltimore Police's gun recovery records. What we saw shocked us.

The Baltimore Banner April 2024

Baltimore’s flashiest developer wanted to expand nationally. Now he’s rethinking everything

That time I investigated my landlord.

The Texas Tribune March 2024

In Texas, natural disasters increase domestic violence risks

This investigation, a partnership between the Texas Tribune, Howard Center for Investigative Journalism and the Fuller Project, explores the link between increasingly common extreme weather events, and trouble for the systems that support domestic violence survivors.

ProPublica Dec. 2021

He Was Filming on His Phone. Then a Deputy Attacked Him and Charged Him With Resisting Arrest.

My first big journalism project. I was exposed to police data for the first time in this investigation into the Jefferson Parish, LA Police Department's use of so-called "cover charges".
